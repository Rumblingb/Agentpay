# AgentPay + Ace - Presenter Script

Use this as speaker notes for the ecosystem-first version of the deck.

## Slide 1

What to say:
- This company should be understood as AgentPay first.
- AgentPay is the trust and execution rail.
- Ace is the flagship product proving that rail live in travel.

What to land:
- Platform company.
- Real modules.
- Real flagship.

## Slide 2

What to say:
- AI can now understand what people want.
- It still cannot safely act on their behalf.
- Identity, permission, payment, verification, and accountability are still missing.

What to land:
- The execution gap is the company.

## Slide 3

What to say:
- The modules matter more than any one interface.
- AgentPay provides identity, policy, execution, verification, and trust.
- Those modules are what make agents commercially usable instead of just impressive.

What to land:
- This is infrastructure, not just product polish.

## Slide 4

What to say:
- Ace is one trust surface on top of AgentPay.
- Team agents and vertical agents can run on the same rails.
- That is why the ecosystem story is credible rather than theoretical.

What to land:
- One platform.
- Multiple surfaces.

## Slide 5

What to say:
- Ace is the living reference implementation.
- It already covers the core delegated travel loop.
- Voice, recommendation, approval, booking, payment, receipt, and recovery are not mockups.

What to land:
- The flagship is materially real.

## Slide 6

What to say:
- Ace matters because it proves trust to humans.
- It gives developers and partners a production reference implementation.
- It sharpens the protocol through real use, not abstract theory.

What to land:
- Ace is not a sidecar brand.
- Ace is the flagship proof.

## Slide 7

What to say:
- The same stack also fits teams doing recurring admin work.
- A manager with 30 operators is a very natural design partner for AgentPay.
- The value is immediate: visibility, policy, proof, payout control, and scorecards.

What to land:
- This is not just travel.
- But it is also not random expansion.

## Slide 8

What to say:
- There are three proof points in the company shape.
- Ace proves consumer trust.
- Operator teams prove enterprise workflow value.
- Partner agents prove platform scale.

What to land:
- Same architecture.
- Different proofs.

## Slide 9

What to say:
- We still keep travel first.
- Ace remains the emotional wedge and flagship priority.
- Operator design partners can run in parallel because they validate the same modules, not a different company.

What to land:
- Focus with leverage.

## Slide 10

What to say:
- Revenue comes in three layers.
- Ace direct revenue.
- Workflow software revenue.
- Protocol revenue later.

What to land:
- Product now.
- Platform compounding later.

## Slide 11

What to say:
- The moat is the seam.
- Real product.
- Shared modules.
- Trust as product behavior.
- Expansion only after proof.

What to land:
- This is harder to replicate than a chat wrapper.

## Slide 12

What to say:
- Replace every placeholder with real numbers before using the deck externally.
- Then frame the raise around the three proof points, not vague ambition.

Suggested wording:
- We are raising [amount] to make Ace world-class in travel, land the first operator design partners on AgentPay, and prepare the rails for the first external agents.

What to avoid:
- generic platform language
- life operating system language
- broad TAM talk without proof

What to land:
- This round is about making the ecosystem story undeniable through proof.

## Slide 13

What to say:
- AgentPay is the trust rail.
- Ace is the living proof.
- Travel comes first because it makes the relationship real.
- The ecosystem compounds because the modules already exist.

Final line:
- Product first. Platform second. Same company. Right order.

## Delivery Rules

- Lead with AgentPay, not as abstract protocol language but as modules.
- Keep Ace framed as flagship proof, not a side note.
- Use the operator wedge carefully: as evidence of module reuse, not as a distraction from travel.
- Say `travel first` often so the story feels disciplined.
- Do not say `we do everything`.
- Do not say `flight scanner`.
