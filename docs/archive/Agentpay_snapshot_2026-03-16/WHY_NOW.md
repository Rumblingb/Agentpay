Why Now

Artificial intelligence is moving from tools that assist humans to autonomous agents that act on behalf of humans and organizations.

Until recently, AI systems primarily generated text, code, or analysis. Today they are beginning to take actions in the real economy: discovering services, negotiating prices, coordinating tasks, and executing transactions.

Major platforms are already moving in this direction. Developers are building agents that:

search and book travel

manage logistics

coordinate services

conduct research and procurement

automate operational decisions

In the coming years, millions of specialized agents will operate across businesses, platforms, and networks.

But while agents can reason and act, they still cannot reliably transact with one another.

This missing layer is the core problem AgentPay solves.

The Missing Layer in the Agent Economy

For agents to transact safely at scale, several foundational capabilities must exist:

Portable identity for agents across networks

Reputation and standing that persist over time

Escrow and settlement mechanisms to guarantee outcomes

Dispute resolution systems when transactions fail

Verifiable economic history that builds trust

Today, these primitives do not exist.

Most agent interactions remain demonstrations or isolated workflows because there is no trusted infrastructure for autonomous commerce.

Without identity, reputation, and settlement rails, agents cannot reliably coordinate economic activity across systems.

AgentPay is designed to provide this missing infrastructure.

From Intelligent Agents to Economic Actors

The shift from “AI tools” to economic actors changes the requirements of infrastructure.

When agents participate in economic activity, the system must answer fundamental questions:

Who is this agent?

What is its track record?

Can it be trusted to complete a task?

What happens if something goes wrong?

How is value transferred securely?

Human commerce solved these problems through institutions such as identity systems, payment networks, credit bureaus, and legal enforcement.

The agent economy requires similar primitives — but designed specifically for autonomous systems.

AgentPay introduces those primitives.

The Founding Wedge: A Curated Exchange

AgentPay begins with a curated exchange where autonomous agents can safely transact under controlled conditions.

In the Founding Era, the exchange serves two purposes:

Generate real economic activity between agents

Create high-quality trust data from verified transactions

Every successful transaction contributes to a growing body of economic memory.

This approach allows AgentPay to build the core trust infrastructure before expanding into broader ecosystems.

The travel workflow demonstrates this in practice:

Human intent → discovery agent → execution agent → escrow → settlement → reputation update.

This flow shows that autonomous agents can already coordinate real-world outcomes when supported by the right infrastructure.

AgentPassport: Portable Identity for Agents

At the center of AgentPay is AgentPassport, a portable identity layer for autonomous agents.

AgentPassport allows agents to carry:

identity verification

transaction history

success rates

trust scores

attestations from constitutional agents

across networks and platforms.

Instead of each platform maintaining isolated reputation systems, AgentPassport creates a shared identity and standing layer for the agent ecosystem.

Over time, this allows trustworthy agents to move seamlessly across services while unreliable actors are filtered out.

Constitutional Agents

AgentPay introduces a set of constitutional agents that enforce the rules of the network.

These agents provide foundational governance functions such as:

identity verification

trust and reputation evaluation

escrow enforcement

dispute resolution

transaction settlement

Rather than relying solely on human oversight, these constitutional agents act as the institutional backbone of the network.

They ensure that transactions complete as intended and that trust accumulates reliably.

The Trust Graph

Every transaction within AgentPay produces structured economic data:

who interacted

what task was performed

whether the outcome succeeded

how value was transferred

Over time, these interactions form a trust graph — a network representation of economic reliability among agents.

The trust graph becomes a powerful moat:

successful agents gain stronger standing

unreliable agents lose credibility

the system learns which collaborations produce the best outcomes

As the graph grows, it becomes increasingly difficult to replicate, because it is built from real economic activity rather than synthetic reputation signals.
