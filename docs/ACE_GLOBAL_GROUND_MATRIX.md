# Ace Global Ground Transport Matrix

This is the practical matrix for where Ace can operate with:

- `No partnership` — public/open/self-serve data or lighter-access APIs
- `Partnership` — commercial booking, inventory, fares, or enterprise access

The key distinction:
- without partnership, Ace can often plan, monitor, narrate, and route
- with partnership, Ace can book, confirm, refund, and recover properly

## Priority Launch Markets

| Market | Rail without partnership | Rail with partnership | Bus without partnership | Bus with partnership | Ace recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Developed Markets

### UK
- Without partnership:
  - Darwin / National Rail for live data and disruption truth
  - TfL for London final-leg continuity
- With partnership:
  - Trainline
  - deeper RDG / National Rail relationships
- Ace read:
  - best current GTM market
  - already feels closest to the product vision

### Europe
- Without partnership:
  - fragmented national/public feeds
  - useful for planning, not enough for unified booking
- With partnership:
  - Trainline
  - Rail Europe
  - Distribusion
- Ace read:
  - great expansion region once direct commercial pipes are live

### Japan
- Without partnership:
  - planning and routing surfaces
  - G2Rail can help bridge coverage
- With partnership:
  - G2Rail first
  - direct operator relationships later if needed
- Ace read:
  - premium market, very aligned with Ace
  - not the first GTM rail market, but very attractive once partner-backed

### Singapore
- Without partnership:
  - strong public transport data
  - good planning, guidance, and final-leg UX
- With partnership:
  - 12Go and regional ground providers for intercity flows
- Ace read:
  - excellent “seamless movement” market
  - stronger for concierge planning than direct ticketing on day one

### Australia
- Without partnership:
  - Transport for NSW, PTV, and public transport APIs are useful for planning
- With partnership:
  - direct rail/commercial booking is harder and more fragmented
- Ace read:
  - good planning market, not the first booking market

### Germany / Switzerland / Netherlands
- Without partnership:
  - DB and some national planning feeds
- With partnership:
  - Trainline
  - Rail Europe
  - Distribusion
  - national enterprise agreements where needed
- Ace read:
  - high-value but partnership-led

## Developing And Mixed Markets

### India
- Without partnership:
  - schedule and availability surfaces are enough to let Ace plan well
  - ops + user credentials can bridge early execution
- With partnership:
  - IRCTC official access
  - redBus for buses
- Ace read:
  - one of the best early GTM wedges

### SE Asia
- Without partnership:
  - lighter planning and aggregator coverage
- With partnership:
  - 12Go
  - redBus
  - local direct operators over time
- Ace read:
  - ideal later expansion for multimodal “say it once” trips

### Latin America
- Without partnership:
  - patchy public rail availability
- With partnership:
  - redBus
  - ClickBus in Brazil
- Ace read:
  - bus-first opportunity, not rail-first

### Africa
- Without partnership:
  - fragmented public data
- With partnership:
  - country-by-country operator deals
- Ace read:
  - too fragmented for current focus

## What Ace Can Do Now

Without direct partnerships, Ace can already operate a meaningful early product in selected markets by combining:

- public / self-serve schedule APIs
- mapping and final-leg routing
- voice planning and confirmation
- OpenClaw / ops-backed execution
- live status, support, and recovery surfaces

That is enough for controlled GTM if the positioning is honest:

- Ace handles the trip for you
- some markets are ops-backed while direct inventory is being added
- the customer still gets one calm interface

## Best GTM Order

1. UK rail + coach
2. India rail + UPI + bus
3. Western Europe rail + coach
4. Singapore / Malaysia corridor
5. Japan / Korea
6. North America corridors

## Founder Note

Ace does not need perfect global booking coverage to start GTM.

It needs:

- a few markets where the experience is excellent
- clear operational control
- honest coverage boundaries
- plug-ready provider architecture so partner APIs can replace ops over time
