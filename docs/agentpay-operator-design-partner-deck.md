# AgentPay - Operator Design Partner Deck

Audience:
- managers of 10 to 50 person recurring admin teams
- billing administration leaders
- travel operations teams
- outsourced workflow operators

## Slide 1 - Cover

### AgentPay for controlled, verifiable operator workflows

Subhead:
- Assign work.
- Verify completion.
- Release payout only when the job is done.

## Slide 2 - The Problem

### Most operator teams still run on chat, spreadsheets, and trust.

Pain:
- unclear ownership
- weak proof of completion
- manual approvals
- messy payout logic
- little trust signal across operators over time

## Slide 3 - What AgentPay Changes

### AgentPay turns work into controlled execution.

Flow:
1. Create work intent
2. Apply policy
3. Capture proof
4. Approve or reject
5. Release payout
6. Update trust score

## Slide 4 - Why This Fits Your Team

### A 30-person admin team is the right size for this model.

Why:
- enough workflow volume to matter
- enough managers for approval logic to matter
- enough operator variance for scoring and routing to matter
- small enough to launch without enterprise procurement drag

## Slide 5 - Example Use Cases

### The same engine fits multiple recurring admin loops.

Examples:
- billing administration
- eligibility and denial follow-up
- payment posting
- travel operations
- procurement support
- other SLA-based back-office work

## Slide 6 - What Is Already Real

### AgentPay is not starting from zero.

Already present in the repo:
- intent creation
- policy controls
- escrow and settlement
- receipts and verification
- trust events and ranking
- public and private workflow surfaces

## Slide 7 - What A Design Partner Gets

### You help shape the first workflow product on top of the rails.

You get:
- direct product access
- founder-led workflow mapping
- early control over the approval and payout model
- a system shaped to your real operations instead of generic SaaS assumptions

## Slide 8 - What We Need From You

### One narrow workflow first.

Ask:
- choose one recurring workflow
- define success and proof
- define who approves
- define payout logic
- run a limited pilot with one manager and one operator group

## Slide 9 - Close

### The point is not more software.

Close line:
- The point is a better operating system for work that needs control, proof, and accountable settlement.
