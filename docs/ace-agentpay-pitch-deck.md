# Ace x AgentPay Pitch Deck

Purpose: a founder-ready pitch deck for early investors, strategic partners, and design partners.

Narrative rule:
- Lead with Ace as the product humans understand.
- Reveal AgentPay as the infrastructure that makes Ace possible.
- Keep travel as the first wedge.
- Do not lead with "life operating system" before travel feels world-class.
- Do not invent metrics. Insert real numbers where marked.

Visual direction:
- Dark navy, cold white, silver, restrained cyan.
- One strong product image per slide.
- Use real app screens, not mockups, wherever possible.
- Keep each slide to one headline and three to four proof points.

## Slide 1 - Cover

On slide:
- Ace
- Travel, handled.
- The trusted companion for the logistics of real life.
- Powered by AgentPay.

Speaker note:
- Ace is the product people meet first.
- It starts with travel, but the company is larger than travel.

Visual:
- Full-bleed Ace face or conversation screen.

## Slide 2 - Problem

Headline:
- Real-life logistics are still fragmented, manual, and reactive.

On slide:
- Search is separate from booking.
- Booking is separate from payment.
- Payment is separate from support.
- When plans change, the customer becomes the workflow engine.

Talk track:
- Travel is the clearest example, but the pattern exists everywhere.
- People do not want another booking app. They want the loop handled.

Visual:
- A simple left-to-right chain:
  search -> compare -> forms -> payment -> disruption -> recovery

## Slide 3 - Why Now

Headline:
- AI can finally understand intent, but it still lacks trusted execution.

On slide:
- Models can understand nuanced requests.
- Consumers are now comfortable speaking naturally to software.
- The missing layer is identity, payment, and trust.
- That gap is where Ace and AgentPay meet.

Talk track:
- The intelligence moment has arrived.
- The execution layer has not.
- Most "AI assistants" stop at suggestion. Ace is built to act.

Visual:
- "Understands" on one side, "Can act safely" on the other, with Ace + AgentPay bridging the gap.

## Slide 4 - Product

Headline:
- Ace is a voice-first travel concierge, not a search UI with a chatbot on top.

On slide:
- Tell Ace where you need to go once.
- Ace finds the strongest route.
- Ace books when you approve.
- Ace stays with the journey when plans change.

Talk track:
- Ace is designed to feel calm, premium, and inevitable.
- The user should feel: I can just ask once.

Visual:
- Converse screen plus receipt or live journey screen.

## Slide 5 - What Exists Today

Headline:
- The product is already materially real.

On slide:
- Voice -> STT -> intent -> plan -> biometric confirm -> payment -> receipt
- Live journey truth: disruption polling, platform changes, and notifications
- Journey recovery: recovery states, reroute logic, and trip continuity
- Shared travel, route memory, railcard awareness, and travel preferences

Talk track:
- This is not a concept deck.
- The architecture already supports the core delegated travel loop.

Visual:
- Three screen sequence:
  conversation -> live journey -> receipt

## Slide 6 - Why Users Trust Ace

Headline:
- Trust is not a policy page. Trust is the product.

On slide:
- One calm surface from request to receipt
- Explicit approval before payment and booking
- Clear receipt, booking reference, and journey continuity
- Ace stays with the trip instead of disappearing after checkout

Talk track:
- Trust comes from behavior, not claims.
- The difference is that Ace does not just suggest. It takes responsibility for the loop.

Visual:
- Receipt screen with reference and journey details.

## Slide 7 - Why Travel First

Headline:
- Travel is the best wedge because the pain is frequent, expensive, and time-sensitive.

On slide:
- High intent and clear willingness to pay
- Real disruption makes delegated recovery valuable
- Repeat routes create memory and habit
- Flights and hotels deepen the same travel wedge

Talk track:
- Travel is big enough to matter and narrow enough to win.
- The goal is not to do everything early. The goal is to become undeniable somewhere first.

Visual:
- Corridor map or simple "rail -> flights -> hotels" wedge diagram.

## Slide 8 - The Bigger Thesis

Headline:
- Ace is the consumer companion. Travel is where that relationship starts.

On slide:
- The pattern is bigger than travel:
- booking
- payment
- reminder
- disruption
- recovery

Examples:
- Train to Edinburgh
- Dentist checkup
- GP appointment
- Boiler service
- Estate agent viewing

Talk track:
- Every new vertical is not "more features."
- It is another proof point that trusted agent execution works in the real world.

Visual:
- Travel in the center, with future rings for health, home, and life logistics.

## Slide 9 - AgentPay Platform Reveal

Headline:
- AgentPay is the infrastructure underneath Ace.

On slide:
- Identity layer: who is acting
- Payment layer: how money moves
- Trust layer: what can be authorised and verified
- Execution layer: how the action is recorded and recoverable

Architecture:

```text
AgentPay Protocol
    |
    |- Ace
    |- Third-party travel agent
    |- Third-party health agent
    |- Third-party domain agents
```

Talk track:
- Ace proves to humans that agents can be trusted.
- AgentPay proves to developers that trusted execution can be built.
- Ace is the flagship consumer experience. AgentPay is the infrastructure standard underneath it.

Visual:
- Clean platform diagram, not a crowded infrastructure chart.

## Slide 10 - Go To Market

Headline:
- Start narrow, high-quality, and controlled.

On slide:
- UK rail and India rail first
- Selected coach and ground-travel corridors next
- Founder-led pilots and premium invite-based rollout
- Direct partner access where possible, ops-backed execution where needed

Talk track:
- We do not need every route in every country to begin learning.
- We need a focused surface where Ace is clearly better than manual travel handling.

Visual:
- Launch phases:
  Phase A: UK rail / India rail
  Phase B: flights and hotels
  Phase C: broader life logistics

## Slide 11 - Business Model

- The company compounds through both product revenue and platform revenue.

On slide:

- Mid term: B2B travel distribution and team travel workflows
- Long term: AgentPay transaction and execution revenue across many agents

Talk track:
- Ace is the visible product relationship.
- AgentPay becomes the infrastructure that earns regardless of which trusted agent executes the task.

Visual:
- Two-layer model:
  Ace on top, AgentPay underneath

## Founder Delivery Notes

- Say "travel first" often.
- Use "trusted execution" more than "AI assistant."
- Do not oversell global coverage.
- Do not position Ace as a flight scanner.
- Do not say "life operating system" before the audience believes the travel wedge.
- Keep the emotional line simple:
  Ace handles the logistics of life so the customer can stay focused on living it.
